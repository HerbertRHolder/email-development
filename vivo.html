Hey Herbert! 

Have you heard about the superfood that is rich in omega 3 fatty acids, essential vitamins and trace minerals; and can only be found underwater?

If you think we’re talking about fish, guess again. Believe it or not; the real nutritional powerhouse of our waters is algae.

There are more than 150 edible species of algae growing in both freshwater and saltwater. Algae is the primary producer of some of the most important nutrients in our diets; including those extra-tricky-to-source ones like omega 3, vitamin D and iodine.

And because it’s much lower down the food chain, algae doesn’t accumulate the same heavy metals and contaminant found in fish. Not only is it kinder to our planet to consume algae; it’s far healthier too!

Here are some of the ways we can all benefit from algae

Omega 3

Fish get their omega 3 by feeding on algae. So rather than rely on fish for our omega 3 consumption; why not go straight to the source?

Our omega 3 supplement is made from the marine algae, Schizochytrium sp, grown in mineral water tanks. This means it avoids potential contamination from oceanic pollutants, and harvesting it doesn’t cause any harm to marine life.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D3 is considered an ‘essential’ supplement in the darker and colder months, because our body just can’t make enough from the available sunlight. But commerical vitamin D3 is derived from lanolin, a by-product of the sheep sheering industry.

Our liquid D3 is also derived from algae, which convert sunlight into vitamin D. How cool is that? We’ve combined it with vitamin K2 for superior absorption, and MCT oil for bioavailability.

Iodine

Algae also provide one of the best known dietary sources of iodine, a mineral that is critical for the health of our thyroid gland. Many widely available sea vegetables are an excellent source of iodine; including sea kelp, nori, and dulse.
